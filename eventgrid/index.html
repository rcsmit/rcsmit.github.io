Questions

Honest answers

Do I need to know how to code?+
Nope. The only technical step is a one-time 15-minute setup — and we provide a guide with screenshots for every single click. On Pulse and Orbit, we just do it for you.
What is this "small connector" in the setup?+
It's a tiny free piece of tech (a Cloudflare Worker, if you're curious) that reads your Google Sheet and feeds it to your calendar. It runs for free, you set it up once and never touch it again. We walk you through it step by step — or we do it for you on paid plans.
How often does the calendar update when I edit the sheet?+
EventGrid polls your Worker endpoint every 5 minutes. So within 5 minutes of saving a row in Google Sheets, it appears on your public calendar. No refresh buttons, no manual publishing.
Can I use my own domain name?+
Yes, you embed the calendar on your own domain.
What are the "3 calendar holders" in Orbit?+
Each holder is a completely independent branded calendar — its own name, logo, colors, domain, and Google Sheet. So you could run a salsa calendar, a bachata calendar, and a festivals-only view from one Orbit account. Great for agencies or multi-discipline communities.
Can I sell sponsored slots to event organizers?+
Yes — on Orbit you get a "Promoted" badge and carousel slots you can offer to organizers. How you price and sell them is entirely up to you. A common approach: charge €5–€20/event for a featured slot. With enough traffic that alone pays your Orbit subscription.
Is there a free trial on paid plans?+
Every paid plan comes with a 14-day free trial, no credit card needed. You get full access — see it live with your real events before paying anything.
